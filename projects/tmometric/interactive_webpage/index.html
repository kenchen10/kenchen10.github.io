Adapting Quality Metrics to Tone Mapping

Supplementary Webpage

This is a webpage meant to supplement the SIGGRAPH 2026 technical paper submission "Adapting Quality Metric to Tone Mapping". Detailed metric evaluation reports, across the 5 tested tone mapping quality assessment datasets and 5 perceptual display encoding transfer functions, are shown here.

The table below is horizontal-scrollable; click the encoding to sort by Spearman correlation scores, with green representing high correlation ranking. Click the metric name to link to scatter plots showing detailed metric predictions vs. subjective quality responses. Colored markers in scatter plots represent results for the same scenes, and dashed lines correspond to the linear fits per-scene.

Datasets:

  • LUNAM TM Image Quality Dataset (Preference of experience in image tone-mapping: Dataset and framework for objective measures comparison [Krasula et al., 2016])
  • Linköping TM HDR Video Dataset (Evaluation of tone mapping operators for HDR video [Eilertsen et al., 2013])
  • LIVE TM HDR Database (Subjective Quality Assessment of Compressed Tone-Mapped High Dynamic Range Videos [Venkataramanan and Bovik, 2024])
  • What is HDR? (What is HDR? Perceptual Impact of Luminance and Contrast in Immersive Displays [Chen et al., 2025])
  • Our Dataset (see Section 4 in the manuscript)

The user study stimuli can be found for each scene: Anime, Bloom, College, Glade, Lounge, Man, Rushmore, Woman, Snow, Toys, Type, Water.
